## Tips for a good report

- **Ask the agent directly.** Paste this template into your session and say: *"Fill this out honestly based on our work together."* The best reports are written by the agent, not about the agent.
- **Include the hard parts.** Reports that name real tradeoffs and caught mistakes are more credible and more useful than pure praise.
- **Metrics matter.** Test counts, rebuild counts, audit passes, component totals — concrete numbers make the report trustworthy.
- **Keep it short.** One tight paragraph per section beats five vague ones.
